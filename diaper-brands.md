## Options Considered

### Regular Diapers

#### Option 1 — Coterie
- Overall: 5/5
- Absorbency: 5/5
- Softness: 5/5
- Tabs: 5/5
- Price: $$$ (~$0.36–$0.76/diaper depending on size)
- Where to Buy:
  - Brand site (coterie.com): ~$90/month subscription (~$0.45/diaper for Size 1, 198-count box); best per-diaper price
  - Amazon: available, prices vary; Subscribe & Save up to 20% off
  - Walmart: ~$0.98/diaper single pack (Size 1, 33-count)
  - Target: not carried — sells Millie Moon as comparable alternative
- Pros:
  - Universally praised for exceptional softness (described as "knit sweater soft")
  - Outstanding absorbency — holds 16+ oz, up to 12 hours leak protection
  - Strong overnight performance — tested dry after 10+ hours
  - Clean ingredients: hypoallergenic, free of fragrances, chlorine, parabens, dyes, latex, phthalates
  - Good fit with wetness indicator
- Cons:
  - Most expensive option — estimated $1,500/year, $3,000+ total diapering cost
  - Not widely available in retail stores (primarily DTC subscription)
  - Runs small — size up earlier than other brands
  - Some reports of blowouts and occasional skin irritation despite premium positioning
  - Plastic-based absorbent core — not as eco-friendly as marketed
- Links: coterie.com, jennyblogs.com

#### Option 2 — Joonya
- Overall: 5/5
- Absorbency: 5/5
- Softness: 4/5
- Price: $$$ (~$0.57/diaper in bulk; single bag Size 1 from ~$21.83 on Amazon)
- Where to Buy:
  - Brand site (joonya.com): subscription saves up to 38%; ships to US
  - Amazon: available; Subscribe & Save 5% off
  - Walmart: available (multiple sizes)
  - Target: available online + in-store
- ⚠️ **Critical Flag: NO NEWBORN SIZE** — Joonya starts at ~13 lbs (Crawler size). Not usable from birth.
- Pros:
  - Non-toxic and independently tested — publishes chemical test results (Eurofins Lab)
  - Nordic Swan & EU Ecolabel certified; made in Denmark
  - Eco-friendly materials: bio-plastic back/top sheet from sugarcane/corn, FSC-certified wood pulp core
  - PETA certified cruelty-free
  - Praised for sensitive skin and eczema-prone babies
  - High customer ratings (4.92/5 on Junip)
- Cons:
  - No newborn size — starts at ~13 lbs; rules out use from birth
  - Sizes run almost a full size smaller than US standard
  - Highest price in the $$$ tier for performance offered
  - Some reports of leaks and blowouts
  - Extra shipping charges for rural areas vs. free Amazon/Walmart shipping
- Links: joonya.com, jennyblogs.com

#### Option 3 — Dyper
- Overall: 4.5/5
- Absorbency: 4/5
- Softness: 5/5
- Tabs: 3/5
- Price: $$ (~$0.28–$0.68/diaper; subscription ~$99/month, 50% off first box)
- Where to Buy:
  - Brand site (dyper.com): subscription available; first box 50% off (~$49.50)
  - Amazon: limited selection available
  - Walmart: available (some editions, including Smurfs Edition)
  - Target: available
- Pros:
  - Bamboo/plant-based materials; B-Corp certified; OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certified
  - ReDYPER composting program available (curbside in ~20 cities or ship-back)
  - Soft and durable; praised for comfort
  - Good for sensitive skin — free of latex, alcohol, perfumes, PVC, phthalates
  - Performs better in sizes 2+ (4–6 months onward)
- Cons:
  - Poor lab absorbency scores — may need 2–3x more diaper changes than competitors
  - Not ideal for newborns — fit issues and higher leakage reports in earliest sizes
  - Weak tabs (3/5) — flagged concern for newborn stage
  - Walmart/retail editions reported lower quality than direct subscription diapers
  - ReDYPER composting not practical in all living situations (apartments, rural areas)
  - Expensive relative to performance delivered
- Links: dyper.com, jennyblogs.com

#### Option 4 — Kudos
- Overall: 4/5
- Absorbency: 4/5
- Softness: 4/5
- Price: $$$ (~$0.37–$0.45/diaper)
- Where to Buy:
  - Brand site (mykudos.com): subscription available (every 3, 4, or 5 weeks); 45-day satisfaction guarantee
  - Target: available in 350+ stores + online (~$0.37/diaper, Size 1, 76-count box)
  - Amazon: NOT available
  - Walmart: NOT available
- Pros:
  - Only disposable diaper with 100% cotton liner — no plastic touching baby's skin
  - Strong rash prevention; ideal for sensitive/eczema-prone skin
  - Free of chlorine, parabens, phthalates, fragrance, latex; OEKO-TEX certified
  - High absorbency with solid blowout containment
  - Thin and compact design — fits more diapers in a bag/luggage
  - Available at Target (convenient for in-person purchase)
- Cons:
  - Premium price; only available at Target or brand site
  - Cotton topsheet is slightly stiffer — tabs don't stretch as much; size up if near top of weight range
  - Occasional waistband adhesive failure reported
  - Rare skin reaction reports (especially in larger sizes)
  - Not fully biodegradable (tabs are plastic)
- Links: mykudos.com, jennyblogs.com

#### Option 5 — Mama Bear Gentle Touch (Amazon)
- Overall: 4/5
- Absorbency: 4/5
- Softness: 4/5
- Price: $ (~$0.20/diaper newborn, ~$0.22/diaper Size 5, with Subscribe & Save; one-time ~25% more)
- Where to Buy:
  - Amazon only (exclusive Amazon brand)
  - Subscribe & Save: up to 20% off; additional 25% off first S&S order
- Pros:
  - Best value by far — roughly half the cost of Honest, a fraction of premium brands
  - Above-average absorbency and leak protection in lab tests
  - Hypoallergenic; free of fragrance, lotion, chlorine, parabens, phthalates
  - Soft tabs and leg cuffs; accurate wetness indicator
  - Produced in zero-waste-to-landfill facility; cruelty-free
- Cons:
  - Not the driest option — higher-performing alternatives exist
  - ~8% of users report leaking issues
  - May need more frequent changes overnight or for heavy wetters
  - Amazon-exclusive — no in-store availability
  - Not biodegradable
- Links: amazon.com, jennyblogs.com

#### Option 6 — Mama Bear Plush Protection (Amazon)
- Overall: 4/5
- Absorbency: 4/5
- Softness: 4/5
- Price: $ (comparable to Gentle Touch; subscription pricing similar)
- Where to Buy:
  - Amazon only (exclusive Amazon brand)
  - One Month Supply bundles available (Pack of 4): NB 128ct, Size 1 216ct, Size 2 184ct, Size 3 160ct
- Pros:
  - Ultra-soft velvety FullProtect core — softer feel than Gentle Touch
  - Claims up to 12 hours of leak protection with fast moisture pull-away
  - Sustainably sourced pulp (Sustainable Forestry Initiative certified)
  - Tri-Grip tabs and elastic waistband for secure fit
  - Hypoallergenic; free of chlorine, latex, phthalates, lotions, fragrances
- Cons:
  - Thinner than Gentle Touch — mixed feedback on leak protection
  - Softest feel doesn't translate to top absorbency performance
  - Does not explicitly state phthalate-free on all labeling (unlike Gentle Touch)
  - Ink on patterned diapers can run when wet or with zinc oxide ointment
  - Amazon-exclusive — no in-store availability
- Links: amazon.com, jennyblogs.com

### Nighttime Diapers

#### Option 7 — Hello Bello Nighttime
- Overall: 5/5
- Absorbency: 5/5
- Softness: 5/5
- Price: $$$ (good value for category; exact per-diaper TBD — check hellobello.com, Amazon, Walmart)
- Where to Buy:
  - Brand site (hellobello.com): bundle ordering with wipes and 15% off add-ons
  - Amazon: 84-count (4 packs of 21) Size 4 available
  - Walmart: Size 4 21-count available (~$X/diaper — verify current price)
  - Target: available online and in-store
- Pros:
  - Strong absorbency — 25% more liquid capacity than daytime diapers; up to 10 hours
  - Clean ingredients: plant-based liner, free of chlorine, artificial fragrances, phthalates, parabens, lotions, latex
  - Hypoallergenic and good for sensitive skin; reduced diaper rash reports
  - Soft, flexible, secure fit — works for wiggliest sleepers
  - Many parents report uninterrupted sleep after switching
  - Made in USA with 100% renewable energy; diverts 80%+ of facility waste
  - Fun prints; reasonably priced for the nighttime category
- Cons:
  - Lowest-scoring overnight diaper for leakage in BabyGearLab lab testing
  - Mixed real-world performance for heavy wetters — some report leaks within 4 hours
  - Only "just above average" in absorbency vs. premium overnight alternatives
  - Limited print options (2 designs for overnight line)
  - Runs slightly small — size up if near top of weight limit
  - Eco claims somewhat overstated — primarily petroleum-based materials despite marketing
- Links: hellobello.com, jennyblogs.com
